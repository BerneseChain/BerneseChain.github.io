What can I do with my $BERNESE?

Just hold it.

Another revenue activity is to pool $BERNESE together with $WBTC (in pool 2038), with $wstETH (in pool 2037), with Gold ($PAXG) (in pool 3001), with USD (in pool 2239) or even get better APRs by predicting closer ranges of sideway moves within the USD concentrated liquidity pool (in pool 2178).

This is the only fair activity for a dog token, as it requires the least amount of attention, user's money is unlocked all the time, and anybody can come and go anytime.

Is it safe? Will it be swept by a single user?

34% of the supply, as of today (August 2025), has been paired with diversified assets

What's $BERNESE added value?

Bernese's value is intangible and subjective. Members of the community have given value to different factors:

  • Community is humble: The airdrop depended on a very wide group of people giving support to a chain that was about to close (proportionally to their stakes).
  • It is just a large breed dog token and might appeal to users that appreciate dogs justs as they are.
  • Dog breed is funny and many memes can come over the years.
  • Every piece of documentation is copyleft and open source, starting from the logo. Authorship reference rights are for 'Bernese Chain Community'
  • There are no founders or gimmicks to artificially play with the price. Creator has no control anymore of the token and has low allocation just as any other user.
  • Social media channels will never ping, or search for attention.
  • Any participant puts their free time: There's no community pool and no need to pay validators, creating NFT's, games, community moderators, lockings, burns or announcements without substance.
  • No risks of bugs, chain halts or IBC channel closures, since Osmosis is a very well maintained chain.

Where is the liquidity located?

Liquidity can be found in Osmosis, in pools 2038, 1700, 2037, 2039, 2239, 2178.

Why no staking, locking, incentives or inflation?

The token depends on the security provided by Osmosis. On each transaction, the user will pay small $osmo tx fees for transfers, swaps or adding liquidity to the pools.
